Part 1: The Oral Microbiome — Your Mouth's Hidden Ecosystem

Your mouth contains over 700 species of bacteria. In a healthy mouth, good bacteria outnumber bad ones roughly 10:1. But modern habits systematically destroy that balance:

  • Antibacterial mouthwash — kills indiscriminately, good and bad
  • Processed sugars — feed harmful acid-producing bacteria
  • Antibiotics — decimate your bacterial population broadly
  • Chronic stress — shifts immune responses and saliva composition

Signs your oral microbiome is imbalanced: persistent bad breath, bleeding gums when you floss, frequent cavities, or a white coating on your tongue.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can ProDentim reverse existing cavities?
No. Once a cavity forms, it requires a dental filling. However, ProDentim can help create an environment that inhibits new cavity formation by reducing harmful acid-producing bacteria.
Is it safe to use with dental implants?
Generally yes, but always consult your dentist or oral surgeon first, especially if you've had implant surgery recently.
Why do I feel worse in the first week?
This is a Herxheimer (die-off) reaction. As harmful bacteria die, they release toxins that temporarily cause symptoms like altered taste or mild discomfort. This passes within 5–7 days and is a sign the probiotic is working.
Can I take it while on antibiotics?
Yes, but space them at least 2 hours apart to prevent the antibiotic from killing the probiotic strains before they can colonize.
What happens if I stop taking it?
Your oral microbiome will gradually return to its previous baseline over 4–8 weeks. The benefits are dependent on continued daily use — similar to how stopping a good diet reverses its effects over time.
